Chirurgeon

Book 1 of The Hambledon Free Company · A Mercenary Progression Fantasy
Out now

One second I had my hands inside a dying man in a Pittsburgh trauma bay. The next, the patient was a woman, the wound was a halberd through the thigh and the bracer on my wrist told me the save had just leveled my Triage to two.

So they keep me close. And three of them keep me closer. One holds the line alone, calls me hers in front of the whole company, and breaks fingers when men forget it. The other decided I was hers the first time she watched me work, and tracks every rival like a hawk. And the last barely speaks, reaches the bodies first, and those who get too close to me have a way of turning up among them.

About Alex

Alex Savage is a pen name. The books are real. The rest is none of your business.

I write action haremlit — overpowered men trying very hard not to be noticed, in worlds that won’t stop noticing them. Expect tradecraft, magic-on-magic violence, single-dad domesticity ruined by inter-dimensional tears, fireballs answered with 9mm, a battlefield chirurgeon’s hands in a world that runs on blood, and women who refuse to let any of it stay quiet.

Three ongoing series, more on the way. If you like your heroes hard-edged, your magic systems with stakes, and your fight scenes with both punchlines and ammunition counts — you’re in the right place.
