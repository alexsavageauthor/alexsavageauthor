# Art brief — Alex Savage hooded figure (rework)

This brief is for the **full painterly illustration** of the Alex Savage personal mark. Hand it to a commissioned illustrator, or use it as a Midjourney/SDXL prompt to iterate.

The simplified SVG silhouette mark (`alex-savage-mark-simple.svg`) handles favicon/avatar/watermark uses. This brief is for the **hero illustration** — the one that lives on the website's about section, press kit, author bio cards, and any place a painterly portrait makes sense.

---

## What's working in the current illustration

Keep:
- The **hooded figure** silhouette — mysterious, character-coded, instantly recognizable
- The **eye-contact moment** with the viewer through the hood shadow — that's the connection
- The **portrait-square aspect ratio** (works as Instagram avatar, profile photo, etc.)
- The general **painterly / hand-drawn quality** — feels deliberately made, not AI-default

## What needs reworking

Replace or refine:

1. **Background clutter.** Current illustration has clockwork gears, intertwined nude figures, and parchment papers all competing for attention. Strip back to **one** atmospheric element — magical sparks, drifting embers, smoke, runes dissolving — that suggests "magic + danger" without diorama.

2. **Color palette.** Current piece is slate-blue/grey on aged parchment. Pivot to the **Alex Savage brand palette**:
   - Background: deep purple `#160C24` blending to surface `#271847`, with hints of `#1F1336`
   - Hood and figure: dark, near-black silhouette with subtle purple undertones
   - **One luminous accent in gold `#E8B756`** — a single eye glint, a magical spark in the hand, a faint glow from inside the hood. The gold should be the only warm element, used sparingly enough that it feels precious.
   - Optional: a single touch of coral `#FF7E94` (a glow, a spell residue) if it earns its place — never both warm accents at full intensity.

3. **Banner / nameplate.** Current piece has "Alex Savage" on a parchment banner at the bottom. **Remove this entirely.** The illustration is the portrait; the name belongs in a separate locked typographic treatment (`alex-savage-wordmark.svg`). Combining them locks the illustration to one use case and prevents it from working as a clean portrait.

4. **Sexual imagery.** The intertwined nude figures in the current background signal haremlit but read as cluttered and reduce the illustration's usability (won't pass Instagram review, can't appear on Amazon author page). The painterly portrait should be **suggestive through atmosphere, not explicit through bodies**. Save explicit imagery for cover art where it's earned by context.

---

## Direction prompt (for Midjourney / SDXL / commissioned brief)

> Painterly portrait illustration of a hooded male figure, three-quarter view, head slightly tilted, one eye catching the light from under the hood with a faint warm gold glint. Background is deep saturated purple gradient (#160C24 to #271847), with a single atmospheric element — drifting magical embers or sparks — in muted gold (#E8B756). Heavy chiaroscuro lighting from below or side, most of the face in shadow, only the gold eye-glint and jaw-line catching light. Painterly digital illustration in the style of a modern dark fantasy book cover artist (think Sam Weber, Tomer Hanuka, or Brom). Composition is portrait-square (1:1). No text, no banners, no extra characters, no background scenery. Mood: mysterious, competent, controlled danger. Avoid grim/grimdark — this is a fantasy operator who knows exactly what he's doing.

**Negative prompts (for AI tools):** text, watermark, signature, banner, scroll, gears, clockwork, nude figures, multiple characters, photorealistic, scenic background, daylight, generic fantasy cliché, anime, cartoon.

## Deliverable specs

When the illustration is final, save in this folder as:

- `alex-savage-portrait-2400.png` — 2400×2400 master (print-quality, transparent or solid bg)
- `alex-savage-portrait-1200.jpg` — 1200×1200 web hero version, JPEG q90
- `alex-savage-portrait-600.webp` — 600×600 web thumb, WebP
- `alex-savage-portrait-400.webp` — 400×400 social avatar size

The simplified SVG mark stays in use everywhere small (≤256px). The painterly portrait takes over wherever the illustration earns its space (website hero, press kit, author bio, interior of book back-matter).

---

## Acceptance criteria

The reworked illustration is approved when it:

- Sits comfortably on `#160C24` without competing with the background
- Reads as "Alex Savage" without needing the name overlaid
- Works at 400px and 2400px without losing the eye-glint as the focal point
- Could appear next to a UA cover or a WA cover without clashing
- Feels like the **author behind** the books, not a character **from** the books
